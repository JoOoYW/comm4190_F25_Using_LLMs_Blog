The Dilemma

I have two very close friends, one currently resides in Virginia and the other in Pittsburgh. They are both six hours away in opposite directions. How am I supposed to shop for them? And UPenn’s so stingy with their breaks that they start their breaks earlier than me, and I work the few days that I even am home! What can I do? But never fear, my experience from our years together and Perplexity will help me find a perfect gift for them.

Their Interests

I have known these two people for at least seven years by now, so I’ve laid out some of their likes, dislikes, and hobbies they have. In addition, I think I want to aim for a budget of at most thirty dollars because gifts are not cheap now (unfortunately).

AI Input

I asked Perplexity the same prompt for both people: you and i are trying to get gifts for our friend, A/J, who likes…, she dislikes…, and her hobbies are …. give me some gifts (single not bundle) that i could give her and are under $30

J’s Possible Gifts

For J, it responded with: Here are some specific under-$30 single gifts that fit J’s interests and avoid her dislikes. #### Burgundy and makeup themed - Burgundy makeup bag (look for burgundy cosmetic or quilted makeup bags on major beauty or department store sites; many are in the $15–$25 range and pair her love of makeup with her favorite color). - Burgundy-toned lip gloss or balm stick (search for burgundy lip gloss or balm from budget or mid-range brands; plenty of single items come in around $8–$15 and feel like a luxe treat without being a set).

Lego-style and craft gifts

  • Small Lego flower set: pick one of the mini botanical or flower-themed building kits that are usually under $30; this nods to her love of flowers and gives her a relaxing build to do at home.
  • Knitting notion set: choose a cute single accessory like a burgundy yarn bowl, penguin stitch markers, or a pretty tape measure; many individual notions are under $20 and tie into her knitting hobby.

Baking and walking themed

  • Cute baking tool: a single decorative spatula, whisk, or silicone baking mold with floral or penguin designs; these are often $10–$20 and add personality to her baking routine.
  • Fun walking accessory: cozy burgundy beanie, ear warmers, or knit gloves for her walks; these are easy to find under $30 and tie in her favorite color while being practical.

Penguin Plush options

  • PomPom Penguin
  • Jellycat Penguin
  • PalmPals Penguin

If you want one top pick that feels very “her,” a mid-size penguin plush plus a burgundy makeup item (like a gloss or small bag) would hit penguins, makeup, and her signature color in one simple gift under budget, especially if you grab them from Target or similar stores where each piece is usually well below $20.

A’s Possible Gifts

For A, it responded with: Here are a few fun, single-item gift ideas under $30 that match A’s favorite things and hobbies.

Cat themed items

  • Coffee Mug with a Cat Lid: Purple ceramic cat mug with lid and straw that hits her love of cats, the color purple, and cozy drinks while she watches HGTV or relaxes after climbing sessions.
  • Unicorn cats chalk bag: Climbing chalk bag covered in unicorn cats, perfect for rock climbing sessions while still nodding to her love of cats.

Chocolates

  • Christopher Elbow Chocolate Assortment: Small box of gourmet artisan chocolates that feels special and indulgent without being a huge, overwhelming box.
  • Ferrero Rocher Assortment: Compact gift box of assorted gourmet chocolates that she can snack on while listening to music or watching HGTV.

Flute shaped jewelry

  • Flute Charm Necklace: Customizable flute charm necklace that can include her initial, making it a personal music-themed keepsake.

Are these good choices?

I think that overall, the selection has some good and bad choices. But I really think the gifts for A were lackluster and honestly kind of childish. If I were to retry this prompt, I would include their ages to get more suitable designs and ideas. In addition, A’s likes, dislikes, and hobbies were less popular or common as gifts (rock climbing the flute specifically) while J’s are definitely more common.
